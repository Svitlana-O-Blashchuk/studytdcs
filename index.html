Study Information

Background & Rationale

Aging affects the human brain, leading to structural and functional alterations with age. However, the exact mechanisms behind these changes remain unclear.

This study aims to investigate how aging influences brain activation patterns during motor tasks. By using advanced neuroimaging and brain stimulation techniques, we seek to understand how neural networks adapt over time. The findings from this research could contribute to the development of interventions to maintain motor function in older adults.

Study Design & Techniques

Neuroimaging (MRI)

Participants will undergo structural and functional MRI scans to examine brain activity and connectivity.

Non-Invasive Brain Stimulation

We use transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) to modulate brain activity and assess its effects on motor performance.

Motor Tasks

Participants will perform simple finger-tapping exercises to measure movement accuracy and speed.

• 20min HD-tDCS stimulation
• 1h 10min MRI (anatomical, spectroscopy, functional)
Sessions 2 & 3 are pseudo-randomized and counterbalanced

Session Details

HD-tDCS: High-Definition transcranial Direct Current Stimulation - a non-invasive brain stimulation technique that uses weak electrical currents to modulate brain activity in targeted regions.

Sham Stimulation: A control condition where electrodes are placed but no active stimulation is delivered, allowing researchers to isolate the specific effects of brain stimulation.

Pseudo-randomized: Sessions 2 and 3 are presented in random order to eliminate sequence effects, with equal numbers of participants receiving each order.

What are we looking at?

Anatomical scans of the brain

These scans give us a detailed 3D image of your brain.

Functional scans of the brain

These scans measure brain activity by detecting changes in blood flow.

Brain neurometabolites

We measure the concentration of neurochemicals in specific regions of the brain.

Motor performance

We will measure how accurate you perform a motor task (finger tapping).

Scientific Output

MaRBEL 2025 Conference

The study methodology was presented at the MaRBEL (Belgian MR) conference held in Liege in February 2025. The presentation received positive feedback from the conference committee, demonstrating the scientific merit and methodological rigor of our research approach.

This conference presentation represents our commitment to transparent scientific communication and sharing our research methodology with the broader neuroimaging community.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our HD-tDCS study

?
Is MRI radioactive?
No, MRI is not radioactive because it uses magnets and radio waves to create images, not radiation like X-rays or CT scans. The procedure is completely safe from a radiation perspective.
?
Is MRI safe?
Yes, MRI is safe. MRI magnets don't affect your body and don't cause harm to tissues or organs. The procedure has been used safely for decades in medical imaging.
?
Can I participate if I have metal implants?
Modern implants are MRI-compatible, so most of them are MRI-safe. However, it is better if you will contact us before signing up for the screening session to make sure that your implant is MRI-safe.
?
Is brain stimulation permanently affects my brain?
No, a single stimulation session is safe. You will not experience any changes in the mood or behavior. The stimulation will temporarily affect your neurochemicals level in motor cortex (~1%), which will be restored after ~60 min and this alteration is not enough to cause change on an individual level.
?
How does brain stimulation work?
The stimulation set-up delivers a small electrical current through the electrodes. This current slightly changes the activity of neurons. It is safe as the current is too weak and reversible as soon as the stimulation is over.
?
Is there a monetary compensation if I participate in the study?
Yes, there is a 60 euros compensation for your time and participation in the complete study.
